Glow Up Diary — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 26, 2026

Glow Up Diary (the "App") is developed and published by Abdul Ahad, operating as Undercover Mysteries ("we," "us," or "our"), based in Pakistan. This policy explains what information the App collects, how it is used, who it is shared with, and the choices you have. By using the App, you agree to the practices described here.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account information

To use the App, you create an account using either an email address and password, or Google Sign-In. Depending on the method you choose, we collect:

  • Your email address
  • Your display name
  • If you sign in with Google, your Google account's profile picture URL

Account authentication is handled by Firebase Authentication, a Google service. We do not see or store your password directly — Firebase manages this on our behalf.

1.2 Your selfie photos

Your selfie photos are uploaded to Cloudinary, a third-party cloud media storage service, so that they are accessible across all your devices. Photos are stored in a private folder tied to your account identifier and are not publicly accessible. Cloudinary's privacy practices are described at cloudinary.com/privacy.

1.3 Selfie metadata stored in our database

In addition to the photo files themselves, the following information about each selfie is stored in our cloud database (Cloud Firestore, a Google service), tied to your account:

  • The date and day number of each selfie you take
  • A reference URL pointing to the photo stored on Cloudinary
  • Your subscription/Pro status and the date your 90-day challenge started

1.4 Purchases and subscriptions

If you purchase a Pro subscription, the transaction itself is processed entirely by Apple's App Store or Google Play — we never see or store your payment card details. To manage entitlements, we use RevenueCat, a third-party subscription management service, which receives your app-assigned user ID and your purchase/subscription history. RevenueCat's own privacy practices are described at revenuecat.com/privacy.

1.5 Advertising

Free-tier use of the App includes ads served by Google AdMob. When an ad is shown, Google's AdMob SDK may collect your device's advertising identifier and ad interaction data, in accordance with Google's own privacy policy. You can read Google's practices at policies.google.com/privacy.

1.6 Information stored only on your device

Your light/dark theme preference and your daily reminder time and on/off setting are stored locally on your device only. Daily reminders are scheduled and delivered entirely on-device — no notification content is ever sent through a remote server.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To create and maintain your account, and let you sign in across sessions and devices
  • To store and display your selfie photos and track your 90-day progress
  • To verify and manage your Pro subscription status
  • To show ads to free-tier users, and to measure ad performance
  • To respond to support requests sent to our contact email
  • To maintain the security and reliability of the App

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use your photos or selfie metadata for advertising targeting.

4. Data Retention and Deletion

We retain your account and app data for as long as your account exists. When you delete your account from within the App:

  • Your account record and selfie metadata are permanently and immediately deleted from our Firestore database
  • Your Firebase Authentication account is permanently deleted
  • Your selfie photo files stored on Cloudinary are scheduled for deletion — because securely deleting files from Cloudinary requires a server-side operation, this may not happen instantly at the moment of account deletion. If you would like to confirm your photo files have been removed, please contact us at the email below and we will process the deletion manually within 30 days
Account deletion cannot be undone. If you need to confirm that all your Cloudinary photo files have been permanently deleted, please email us directly.

5. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights including the ability to access, correct, export, or delete your personal information. You can exercise most of these directly in the App, or by contacting us using the details below.

If you are in the European Economic Area or UK, you have rights under the GDPR. If you are a California resident, you have rights under the CCPA/CPRA, including the right to know what is collected and to request deletion. We do not sell personal information as defined under CCPA.

6. International Data Transfers

Our infrastructure providers (Google/Firebase and Cloudinary) may process and store data on servers located outside your home country. By using the App, you understand your information may be transferred to and processed in countries with different data protection laws than your own.

7. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it.

8. Security

We rely on Firebase's and Cloudinary's built-in security infrastructure to protect your data. Photos are stored in private, access-controlled folders on Cloudinary. No method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the App after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
